Rabbits

"The Furry Wonders: Exploring the Fascinating World of Rabbits"

Beneath the boughs and in the field,
A secret world, so small concealed.
A world where bunnies, soft and fleet,
Bound among the grass and wheat.

Their cotton tails a fluffy sight,
As they play beneath the light.
With eyes that gleam like polished stone,
They hop and dance, and call it home.

They nibble on the clover green,
A sight so pure, a pastoral scene.
Their little noses twitch and twitch,
As they forage, without a hitch.

With fur as soft as angel's wings,
They hop about, a joy that sings.
Their gentle grace, a gift to see,
A wonder in their majesty.

Oh, rabbits dear, so quick and shy,
A little world, you occupy.
Beneath the boughs and in the field,
A secret world, so small concealed.
